E-HEALTH
Role: UX research, visuals, interaction Duration: May-Jully (3 months)

Project Vision

E-Health is a mobile App designed to help hospital patients to estimate services Bill evrywhere. It’s strives all the hospital existing services and the cost of the Bill for each service.

Challenges

1. Design an App accessible for all type of users

2. Provide a seamless and linear purchasing experience

3. Create a minimalistic UI while keeping products as the focused

Kickoff

in this project, we took a goal-directed design approach to be quite effective in our design efforts. we found qualitative research methods to be the most useful. conisting of a kickkoff meeting, literature review, competitive analysis, stakeholder interviewa, and most important our persona hypothesis construction. we started out by asking ourselves initial key questions.

"What is the product and who is it for ?"

"What do our primary users need more?"

"Which users are the most important to the bussiness?"

"What challenge could we face moving forward?"

"Who do we see as our biggest competitors?".

"What literature should we review to familiarize ourselves?"

Meet the Users

PRIMARY

Name: Mick Hilton
Age: 22
Occupation: Data scientist

Mikey works at a marina and loves his job. He’s very passionate about his job but things can be very frustrating about his position. He's been working there for a while and people come and go and he's usually the one training the new guys. The problem is the workers never stay long so he feels he's wasting his time.

SECONDARY

Name: Lidya Mongera
Age: 29
Occupation: Sales

Lidya is an employee at a multinational corporation. She works closely with her colleagues and spend most of her time in team meetings and working on projects. They often works weekends when the staff is huge. However, Lidya is usually taking doctors advices to keep good healthy. being able to estimate her billings online is a time saving for her.
SUPPLEMENTARY
Name: Abdoul Maiga
Age: 35
Occupation: Teacher

Abdoul is a Teacher at a large school. He has a hectic and demanding schedule that does not allow him much personal time and energy to visit hospitals for just a billing estimation. Abdoul is visually-impaired and requires the aid of screen reader technologies. However, not all apps and websites support screen reader usage. Also, he feels that hospital bill estimation apps should include online consultation and online payments.

Wireflow

after sketching out some P&P wireframes and thinking through the preliminary flow, we reviewed what was necessary, and unnecessary and what areas needed improvement. we poured a lot of our time our time into this step to make sure we had the finishing touches on the underlying UX before moving onto the visuals

Initial Usability study

Early designs didn’t allowed for custom research, but after the usability studies, I added an input option to make research. I also revised the design to respect a hierarchy scheme when they first land on the screen.

Second usability study

The second usability study revealed frustration with the login page. The contrast of the connection button is eyes traumatizing, Thus I changed the contrast of the button and made it bigger. And the subscription link is hardly visible, thus, I made it more visible.

Style Guide

combining incredibly vivid colors to create E-health signature gradient came from wanting to express to users just how creative the keyboard hobby can be. The splash the screen feels warm and welcoming enough to draw the user further in it's also the only place where it's applicable to use this much color considering our colorful product selection within the app

Final Designs

Takeaways

E-HEALTH have been inspired by the real social need. I wanted to communicate the importance expressing yourself through different creative outlets. This was my first time using the goal-directed design process , and I can definitely see it being useful in future projects. The idea of honing in on the persona hypothesis creation to help further the goals of not only the user, but the business is a step that I had taken for granted up until now. I learned that if you design for exclusively the business' goals, it will most likely fail; this seems to be especially true in shopping apps.
